# Midnight Weekly Profession Treasure Drought

This document tracks player reports and reference sources about Midnight's weekly profession-knowledge treasure behavior, especially cases where very few repeatable treasures appear to be active at a time.

## Current Summary

- I found no official Blizzard note confirming a change to treasure spawn/respawn rules.
- Blizzard did officially grant extra profession knowledge for the first full launch week, but not a treasure behavior change.
- There are multiple Blizzard forum threads with repeated reports of:
  - flying 20-60 minutes with few or no visible treasures
  - treasures appearing in bursts and then disappearing for long stretches
  - shard/phasing/story-progress differences between characters
  - minimap/icon visibility issues, especially after another player taps a treasure

## Official / Reference Sources

### Blizzard Hotfix Coverage

- Icy Veins summary of Blizzard hotfixes, Feb 23 - Mar 2:
  - https://www.icy-veins.com/wow/news/class-fixes-and-tuning-item-requirements-profession-knowledge-housing-prey-system-weekly-midnight-hotfix-summary-feb-23-mar-2/
  - Relevant profession note:
    - "To account for the acquisition of Knowledge in Early Access, two weeks' worth of Knowledge will be granted to players from activities (Crafting Orders, treasure drops, Patron Orders, gathering) during the first full week of Midnight (March 3-10)."
  - This confirms a knowledge-availability adjustment, not a treasure spawn/respawn rule change.

### Guide Reference For Intended Behavior

- Method guide: All Profession Knowledge Point Sources in Midnight
  - https://www.method.gg/guides/all-profession-knowledge-point-sources-in-midnight
  - Relevant guidance:
    - weekly crafting profession points still come from regular treasures
    - the guide states these can come from treasures "in any of the Midnight zones"
  - This is useful as a baseline when comparing player reports that certain zones feel inactive or much worse than others.

## Patterns Reported By Players

These are not confirmed by Blizzard, but they recur often enough to track:

- Treasures may appear in bursts, then go absent for 10-30+ minutes.
- Zone quality may differ sharply by shard.
- `Zul'Aman` and `Voidstorm` are the most commonly recommended zones.
- `Eversong` is often reported as having more visible treasure interactions but worse profession KP drop rates.
- `Harandar` gets mixed reports and may be more sensitive to phasing.
- Flying low and using `Steady Flight` appears to help many players see treasure minimap icons.
- Some players report minimap icons disappearing once another player interacts with a treasure, even if the treasure remains lootable briefly.
- Low-level or less-progressed alts may see substantially fewer treasures than fully progressed level 90 characters.
